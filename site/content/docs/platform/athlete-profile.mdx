<Info>Beyond **Personal info**, which sections and fields a card holds — and who edits each

The card replaces the scattered spreadsheets of contacts, sizes, and administrative records, and stays current because both coach and athlete can keep it up to date.

## The card

Open **Athlete profiles** and select an athlete to load their card. **Personal info** is the one section always present, with seven fields:

<ParamField path="First name">Edited in place with the pencil.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Last name">Edited in place with the pencil.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Email">The login identity. Read-only in this instance — no pencil.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Date of birth">Set during registration, correctable here with the pencil.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Gender">A code-list value, edited with the pencil.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Status">A code-list value (for example **Active**). Read-only in this instance — no pencil.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Notes">Free text, edited with the pencil.</ParamField>

**Edit in place.** Click the pencil next to a field, change the value, and save. Whether a given field can be edited depends on your team's configuration.

<Note>Which Personal info fields are read-only is set per team. In the demo instance
**Email** and **Status** carry no pencil for either coaches or athletes; elsewhere the
read-only set may differ.</Note>

## Your team's sections

Beyond Personal info, the card can carry additional sections — each a panel of fields using the same in-place pencil pattern. The sections, fields, labels, types, and edit permissions are all configured for your team, so the examples below are illustrative rather than universal.

<ParamField path="Sports info">Sport club, sport classification, and equipment specifics such as ski or pole length.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Equipment & sizes">Shirt, short, and shoe sizes — typically left athlete-editable so they stay current.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Body measurements">Physical measurements kept on the card.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Contacts">Emergency or parent contacts — phone, address, contact name.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="Documents">Personal and administrative records — passport number and validity, anti-doping certificates, attached files.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="External IDs">Values such as an IBU ID that link the athlete to a federation database, so Yarmill records join up with the outside systems already in use — and results can be imported back.</ParamField>

<Info>These sections are not present on every card. Your instance may configure some, all,
or none of them, in whatever order suits how your team works.</Info>

## Who can edit what

**Coaches edit every card.** Coaches see and edit every athlete's card across the group, and can correct any editable field — a mis-spelled name, an old club — directly from the card. The fix then shows up wherever that detail appears.

**Athlete editing is per-field, not a blanket switch.** Keeping the card current is a shared job, so a team typically leaves the details athletes know best — sizes, a renewed passport — open to them, while locking identity and administrative fields.

## What athletes see

Athletes open **Athlete profiles** to find **only their own** card — no group panel and no athlete switcher. Within it, they edit the fields their team has marked athlete-editable. In the demo instance that's **First name**, **Last name**, **Date of birth**, **Gender**, and **Notes**; **Email** and **Status** are read-only.

<Tip>Filling your card is the first thing to do in a new account, alongside
[connecting your devices](/en/platform/integrations) and setting your heart-rate zones.</Tip>

## How it connects

The card is the team's single record for each person, and it reaches across Yarmill:

- **Identity everywhere.** Name, avatar, and date of birth from the card identify the
  athlete in every module — Plan, Reality, [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics), and the rest.
- **External IDs link outside databases.** An IBU ID or similar lets
  [Results](/en/results/results) import federation results against the right athlete.
- **First steps in a new account.** Filling the card is step one of onboarding, paired with
  [connecting devices](/en/platform/integrations) and setting heart-rate zones.

<Note>Device connections (Apple Health, Garmin, Oura, WHOOP, and others) and heart-rate
zones are not on the card — they live under **Settings → Personal**. See
[Integrations](/en/platform/integrations).</Note>
