<Update label="June 16, 2026" title="Yollanda reads your Tabular data">
  {/* NOTE(yarmill): placeholder hero — real visual later via yarmill-visuals. */}
  <Frame caption="Yollanda answering with figures pulled straight from a Tabular view.">
    <img src="/images/changelog/_placeholder.svg" alt="Yollanda referencing an athlete's Tabular data in her answer." />
  </Frame>

  Much of what a coach tracks lives in Tabular — training entries, results, body composition, the day-to-day numbers you type in by hand. Until now [Yollanda](/en/platform/yollanda) couldn't see any of it, so questions about those figures came back thin.

  Now she reads your Tabular data, and this is live across every instance. Ask about an athlete's recent sessions, a results table, or a metric you log there, and Yollanda answers from the actual numbers instead of guessing around them.

  <Info>
    Tabular connection is enabled per instance. It is now switched on everywhere, but the exact tables Yollanda can see depend on how your Tabular is configured.
  </Info>

  ## Snappier answers

  Yollanda now caches the work behind repeated questions, so common asks come back faster and cost less to run. Her chat also recovers on its own when a response stalls mid-stream, instead of hanging until you give up and reload.

  ### Improvements

  - **Wellness questionnaire** — The [questionnaire](/en/reality/wellness-questionnaire) reads more clearly: mood now uses expression icons, soreness and fatigue carry descriptive labels, questions sit grouped under their topics, and the Tabular overview shows both the number and its meaning.
  - **Reporting** — Data from the Athlete profiles, Medical, and OKR modules now flows into reporting, so those areas can be analysed alongside the rest of an athlete's record.
  - **Biathlon** — Shooting visuals that trend over time (item time, time to first shot) now include the hold, bringing them in line with the rest of the framework's shooting charts.
  - **Body composition** — The Tanita report gained labelled Y-axes and now uses the standard framework visuals, matching the InBody report it sits beside.
  - Plus a range of smaller improvements across the app.

  ### Fixes

  - **WHOOP** — Data processing errors that left some athletes with missing or stalled WHOOP data are resolved, and the affected gaps have been backfilled.
  - **Apple Watch** — Training duration and the time counted in heart-rate zones now agree, so a session's summary no longer shows a shorter span than the workout actually ran.
  - **Yollanda** — Output failures that occasionally broke an answer are fixed.
  - **Medical** — The group overview no longer drops an athlete whose account wasn't tied to a specific instance.
  - **Analytics** — The status dot in the analytical-framework view now sits at the position its value implies, so a reading just inside or outside the norm reads correctly at a glance.
  - Plus a number of smaller fixes and performance improvements.
</Update>
