Goals turn season ambitions into something you can track. Each goal holds a set of
**key results** — measurable outcomes with a start, current, and target value — so
progress stays visible all season, not just judged at the end.

**For:** coaches, athletes · **Where:** Web app

<Info>Your team's Yarmill is configured for your sport, so the goal **categories** and
any completeness requirements (for example, a minimum number of key results) may differ
from this guide. The mechanics work the same way.</Info>

## Where to find Goals

Open **Goals** from the main menu. As an **athlete**, you'll see your own goals straight
away. As a **coach**, choose a **group** and then an athlete to work with their goals —
or open your own Goals to set objectives for yourself, useful for your own coaching or
development.

Goals are grouped by season — **Current season**, **Last season**, and **Other** — so
older objectives stay out of the way without being lost.

## Create a goal

A goal is a single objective ("Lift 150 kg on bench press," "Reach the World
Championship final"). You'll give it a name and some context, then break it into key
results.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Goals and pick the person">
    Select the athlete whose goal you're creating — or yourself, as a coach.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new goal">
    Select **+** at the top of the goals list. A **New goal** appears, ready to edit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the goal">
    Give it a title and description, then set its attributes (see below) and add key
    results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Goal fields

<ParamField path="Title" required>
  The objective, in a few words — for example, "Lift 150 kg on bench press."
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Description">
  Free-text context: what success looks like and by when.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="State" default="Not started">
  The goal's status: **Not started**, **On track**, **Off track**, **Completed**,
  **Canceled**, or **Failed**.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Season" default="Current season">
  The season the goal belongs to. Goals are grouped by season in the list.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Priority">
  **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Supervisors">
  The coaches who follow the goal.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Category">
  A two-level label — a category and its subcategory (for example, *Fitness → Strength*).
  The available categories are configured for your team.
</ParamField>

## Add key results

A **key result** is a measurable outcome that tells you whether the goal is being met.
Add as many as the goal needs — each row in the table is one outcome.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name the key result">
    In the key results table, select **Name the key result** and type it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the values">
    Fill in **Start value**, **Current value**, and **Target value**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a target date">
    Select **Set date** and choose when the key result should be reached.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Key result fields

<ParamField path="Name" required>
  The measurable outcome — for example, "Train at least 3× per week."
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Start value">
  Where the athlete began. Free text — a number (`0`) or a phrase (`unknown`).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Current value">
  Where the athlete is now. Updated **manually** as progress is made.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Target value">
  The value that counts as success — for example, `140kg` or `3`.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Target date">
  When the key result should be reached.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="State">
  The key result's status — the same six states as the goal.
</ParamField>

## Track progress

Values are updated **manually**, so keep them current as the athlete progresses — the
analytics and conversations around a goal are only as good as its latest numbers. Update
the **current value** of each key result, and move the goal's **state** as the picture
changes.

Every change — a new value, a state change, a moved target date — is recorded
automatically under **Activity**, with who made it and when.

## Comments and final evaluations

Use **Activity** to leave a comment at any time. Comments are plain text, timestamped,
and a good way for an athlete and coach to talk through progress in context.

When a goal wraps up, add a **final evaluation** — a short closing reflection on how it
went. Both the athlete and a coach can add their own.

<Tip>Goals are at their best in pre- and post-season conversations: set them together at
the start of the season, and evaluate them together at the end.</Tip>

## Check a goal against your methodology

The check icon at the top right opens **Document check** — it flags what's still missing
(red) or recommended (amber) on the goal, with green checks for what's done, and links to
**Open methodology**, your team's methodology document.

<Info>What counts as required or recommended — such as a minimum number of key results —
comes from your team's configured methodology and differs between teams.</Info>

## See a whole group's goals

Select **Entire Group** to see every athlete's goals as a progress grid. Hover a goal
for a quick summary, or select it to open the full view — a fast way to scan a squad
before a planning meeting or review.
