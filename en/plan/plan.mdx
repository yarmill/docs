The Plan is where you decide what training should happen — from the shape of the whole
season down to Tuesday morning's session — and publish it to your athletes when it's ready.
You build it top-down: the same season is available at four zoom levels, so you can set
season-level intentions and then descend to day-by-day detail without leaving the module.

**For:** coaches · **Where:** Web app

<Info>Yarmill is configured for each team, so the training phases on the text side and the
data rows on the numbers side may differ from this guide — those labels are set up for your
instance. The mechanics — the four views, the group sandbox, and Copy plan — work the same
way everywhere.</Info>

## The four views

Plan gives you the same season at four zoom levels, switched at the top of the page. Each
day's plan has two sides: a **text side** in your own words (the configured training phases,
such as *daily goal*, *morning*, *afternoon*) and a **data side** of numbers (minutes, km,
reps, intensity zones, and so on). Some data rows are auto-calculated and locked.

<ParamField path="Week">
  The working view: stacked day cards on the left, each split into the configured training
  phases, and a weekly data grid on the right with columns **Mon–Sun + Total**. Auto-calculated
  rows and the **Total** column are locked. This is where you write each day in detail.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Goals">
  Four consecutive weeks side by side, for comparing the same days across a mesocycle. The
  left row labels are **Cycle goal**, **Weekly goal**, then **Monday–Sunday**. This is a
  different lens on the same plan, not a separate data set. (Distinct from the
  [Goals module](/en/plan/goals).)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Top-down">
  The whole season in three columns — **Mesocycles**, **Microcycles (Weeks)**, and **Days** —
  each cell a free-text theme or main goal. Start here at season start to give the year a
  shape. A settings control configures the cycle structure, and a year selector picks the season.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Annual plan">
  The season-start intention, entered as a grid of your team's data rows against the months
  **January–December + Total**. This is what the Season review later compares your plan and
  reality against.
</ParamField>

<Note>The publish button is labelled **Copy plan** in the Week and Annual plan views, and
**Copy goals** in the Goals and Top-down views. It is the same publish action either way.</Note>

## Your sandbox, and publishing

Planning on the **Entire Group** is your private sandbox — athletes see nothing until you
publish. That's deliberate: sketch freely, then release the week when it's ready.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Plan the week on the group">
    Select **Entire Group**, open the week, and write the days — text on the left, numbers on
    the right. Everything auto-saves; there's no Save button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Copy plan dialog">
    Choose **Copy plan** (top right). A dialog opens, headed *Copy plan &lt;Group&gt; to …*.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose what and who">
    Set the **destination** (athletes in the group, another group, or another week), pick the
    **source days** to include, decide whether to **copy the weekly goal**, and select which
    **athletes** receive it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Confirm in the dialog. Your athletes now see the week as planned.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The dialog has these controls:

<ParamField path="Destination">
  Where the week goes. Defaults to the **athletes in the group**; you can also copy to
  **another group** or **another week**.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Select source days">
  A per-day toggle row (Mon–Sun), all on by default. Turn days off to hold back ones still in
  flux — for example, publish Mon–Thu and keep Fri–Sun private until they're finished.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Copy the weekly goal">
  A checkbox, on by default — include the week's goal text alongside the days.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Select athletes">
  A per-athlete checkbox list of who receives the week, with a **Clear selection** link to
  start over.
</ParamField>

<Tip>Publish early, then individualize. Selecting one athlete and editing their plan changes
only their copy — and is visible to them immediately, no publishing needed.</Tip>

## Individualize after publishing

Select a single athlete in the left list and edit their week — tailor load, swap activities,
adjust days. Only their plan changes, and the change is visible to them immediately, with no
publishing step. From an individual you can also copy a plan back up to the group, or across
to specific teammates, with **Copy plan**.

## Events from the Season Calendar

Camps, races, and testing days planned in the [Season Calendar](/en/plan/season-calendar)
appear automatically in the Plan views on their dates, so each week always carries its context.

<Warning>Calendar events are locked here — they show as chips on their day but can't be edited
in Plan. Change them in the [Season Calendar](/en/plan/season-calendar).</Warning>

## How it connects

The Plan sits at the centre of the planning loop:

- Events from the [Season Calendar](/en/plan/season-calendar) flow into every Plan view on
  their dates and are locked here — edited only in the calendar.
- The plan is the reference your athletes log against in
  [Reality / Training Log](/en/reality/training-log); where it's enabled, **Import plan** lets
  an athlete start a day's diary from the planned day.
- Season objectives live in the [Goals module](/en/plan/goals) — adjacent to the Plan, but
  distinct from the Goals view above.
- Plan-vs-reality trends and the Training Load forecast in [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics)
  use the planned week (dashed line = plan, solid = reality).
