Analytics is where the diary pays off: the data from Plan, Reality, devices, wellness,
and Medical comes back as answers — is the load right, is the athlete recovering, did
the season go to plan.

**For:** coaches (athletes see their own) · **Where:** Web app

<Info>Which analyses you see depends on your team's setup. If an analysis is missing,
it's usually one of three things: the module isn't enabled, you don't have permission,
or the input data isn't being collected — ask your administrator.</Info>

## Team daily readiness

The morning overview: one row per athlete combining **health status** (from the
Medical module), **device recovery** (for example WHOOP recovery or Garmin Body
Battery), and **wellness** — with notes from athletes surfaced. Open it before
training; it tells you who needs a conversation.

## Weekly dashboard

The coach's operational view of who actually filled their diary for a week or
microcycle, at a glance. Each athlete-day carries a symbol — completed as planned, an
athlete note to address, an unfilled diary, a day off, or an illness/injury flag — so
you can see where to follow up. Turn on the **weekly email** to get the dashboard in
your inbox every Monday morning.

## Training load analysis

The load trend for an athlete, with plan and reality side by side — **dashed for plan,
solid for reality**, plus a short **forecast** from the planned week. The load metric is
configurable per team (for example **sRPE**, **TRIMP**, or total training time). The
centerpiece is **ACWR** — the acute:chronic workload ratio, average load over the last
7 days divided by the last 28 — charted against the **0.8–1.3 safe zone**: below it
undertraining, above it elevated injury risk. Summary cards show the current ACWR, time
in the safe zone, and the last week's load and recovery totals.

## Recovery indicators analysis

Recovery metrics from connected devices over the selected period — for example
**resting heart rate** with its 7-day rolling average against the period average,
with illness and injury periods highlighted. Needs a device the athlete sleeps with.

## Wellness

The wellness questionnaire, trended: each metric (sleep, stress, mood, soreness,
fatigue) for the selected day and across a **four-week trend**, per athlete or for the
group, with the athletes' notes alongside.

## Season review

The annual cycle evaluation: **annual plan, plan, and reality** side by side, month by
month, with season totals — switchable to a multi-season **Trend** view, and
exportable. This is where "did we train what we intended?" gets a factual answer.

## Yollanda on charts

Charts and summary cards carry a small Yollanda icon — select it for a plain-language
description and interpretation of what the chart shows. Insights are computed
overnight, so a newly enabled chart explains itself from the next day.
