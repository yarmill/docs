Analytics is where the diary pays off: the data from Plan, Reality, connected devices,
wellness, and Medical comes back as answers — is the load right, is the athlete
recovering, did the season go to plan.

**For:** coaches (athletes see their own) · **Where:** Web app

<Info>Analytics is a **set of reports configured per team and sport**. Which reports you
see depends on your setup, and if one is missing it's usually one of three things: the
module isn't enabled, you don't have permission, or the input data isn't being collected
— ask your administrator.</Info>

## How a report works

Open the **Analytics** menu and pick a report. Each report opens into the same layout:

- a left **report picker** listing the report and its **variants** (some reports ship more
  than one — for example a base group dashboard and a sport-testers variant), each with a
  one-line description;
- a **Filters** block beneath it — a **Group** dropdown on group reports, or **Athlete**,
  **Season**, and **Trend detail** (for example *Months*) on a per-athlete trend report;
- a top bar with a **week or period navigator** (for example ◀ 15/06 – 21/06/2026 ▶), and
  top-right an **email/share** control and an **export/download** dropdown;
- then the **report body** — KPI cards, charts, grids, and tables.

The exact report list, variants, KPIs, and chart set vary per instance. Two report shapes
are described below; your team may have more.

## Team daily readiness

The morning team overview. It combines, for each athlete, the **health status** from the
[Medical module](/en/medical/medical-module) (the training-limitation flag), **device
recovery** (for example WHOOP recovery or Garmin Body Battery), and **wellness** from the
[wellness questionnaire](/en/reality/wellness-questionnaire) — with notes from athletes
surfaced. Open it before training; it tells you who needs a conversation.

The device columns depend on what each athlete has connected — see
[Integrations](/en/platform/integrations).

## Weekly dashboard

The coach's operational view of the group's week — who's at risk, who logged, who didn't,
read at a glance. Pick the **Group** and step through weeks with the navigator. The report
has three parts:

- **Group status** — a row of stat cards counting the athletes who logged at least one day
  of illness or injury, the **comments** athletes wrote during the week, and the
  **skippers** who didn't fill in Reality.
- **Weekly overview of athletes** — a grid of athletes (rows) against **Mon–Sun**, showing
  each day's training content with a legend link, plus a parallel **RPE** grid.
- **Comments table** — the week's notes by **day, athlete, note, and edited** date.

Where it's enabled, turn on the **weekly email** to get the dashboard in your inbox every
Monday morning.

## Training load analysis

The load trend for an athlete, with plan and reality side by side — **dashed for plan,
solid for reality**, plus a short **forecast** from the planned week. The load metric is
configured per team (for example **sRPE**, **TRIMP**, or total training time).

The centrepiece is **ACWR** — the acute:chronic workload ratio, average load over the last
7 days divided by the last 28 — charted against the **0.8–1.3 safe zone**: below it
undertraining, above it elevated injury risk. Summary cards show the current ACWR, time in
the safe zone, and the last week's load and recovery totals. Illness and injury periods
from the [Medical module](/en/medical/medical-module) are shaded into the chart background
so trends are read in health context.

## Recovery indicators

Recovery metrics from connected devices over the selected period — for example **resting
heart rate**, **HRV**, **sleep duration**, **skin-temperature deviation**, or a device
readiness score — each typically shown with a **7-day rolling average** against the period
average, with illness and injury periods shaded in. This analysis needs a device the
athlete sleeps with; without one it's empty. See
[Integrations](/en/platform/integrations) for connecting devices.

## Wellness

The [wellness questionnaire](/en/reality/wellness-questionnaire), trended: each metric (for
example sleep, stress, mood, soreness, fatigue) for the selected day and across a **four-week
trend**, per athlete or for the group, with the athletes' notes alongside. The exact items
depend on how your team configured the questionnaire.

## Training data analysis

Per-athlete training-volume trends — plan vs reality, by activity and intensity — pulling in
data from [Reality](/en/reality/training-log) and connected devices. Set the **Athlete**,
**Season**, and **Trend detail** (for example *Months*). The report opens with:

- a **KPI cards** row — for example training days, sessions, days of illness and health
  restrictions, exercise hours, and total distance;
- **monthly trend charts** — training days and restriction days, exercise hours (evidence vs
  plan), and an exercise-hours decomposition by activity;
- a **sport-tester data** section — *time by activities* and *time by heart-rate zones* from
  Apple, Garmin, Polar, and Suunto devices;
- a **WHOOP data** section — *time by activities* and *time by HR zones*.

Device sections are empty for athletes without that device connected. The exact KPIs and
charts vary per instance and sport.

## Season review

The annual cycle evaluation: **annual plan, plan, and reality** side by side, month by
month, with season totals — switchable to a multi-season **Trend** view, and exportable to
XLS or PDF. This is where "did we train what we intended?" gets a factual answer for the
season debrief. It draws on the plan from [Plan](/en/plan/plan) against logged
[Reality](/en/reality/training-log), and connects to season
[results](/en/results/results).

## Yollanda on charts

Charts and summary cards carry a small Yollanda icon — select it for a plain-language
description and interpretation of what the chart shows. Insights are computed overnight, so
a newly enabled chart explains itself from the next day. For natural-language questions over
the same data, see [Yollanda](/en/platform/yollanda).

## What athletes see

Athletes get a **reduced, self-scoped subset** of the reports — their own data only, with no
group dashboards and no athlete switcher. In the demo instance the athlete menu shows
**three** reports (load and readiness, training data analysis, and the season review); the
group **Weekly dashboard** is absent. The athlete's training data analysis is the same report
with the **Filters scoped to themselves** (Athlete = self, Season, Trend detail) and the same
KPI cards and monthly trend charts, including device data. Coaches keep the full group reports
and team variants. Which reports and charts appear still depends on team setup.

## How it connects

Analytics is the output layer — it reads from every collection module and produces no source
data of its own:

- The **training limitation** from the [Medical module](/en/medical/medical-module) drives
  Team daily readiness, and illness/injury periods are shaded into the load and recovery
  charts.
- Planned load from [Plan](/en/plan/plan) is the **dashed** line and the forecast; logged
  [Reality](/en/reality/training-log) and sport-watch data are the **solid** reality line,
  the time-in-zones, and the recovery metrics.
- The [wellness questionnaire](/en/reality/wellness-questionnaire) feeds the Wellness trend
  and the readiness wellness column.
- [Integrations](/en/platform/integrations) connect the devices behind recovery and
  sport-tester/WHOOP data.
- [Yollanda](/en/platform/yollanda) explains the charts and answers questions over the same
  data.
