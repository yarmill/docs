Connect a device once and Yarmill takes it from there: recorded activities appear under
the right day in your Training Log, and sleep and recovery data feed the readiness
analytics.

**For:** athletes · **Where:** Web app; Apple Health from the iOS app

## Connect a device

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Applications & devices">
    Go to **Settings → Applications & devices**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Connect">
    Choose your provider and select **Connect**, then confirm in the provider's
    account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your Training Log">
    From the next sync, activities (and sleep, if you wear the device at night) appear
    automatically under the day.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Athletes can self-connect, for example: **Garmin**, **Polar**, **Suunto**, **Apple
Health** (Apple Watch), **WHOOP**, **Oura**, and **Dexcom**.

<Info>**Apple Health connects from the iPhone**, not the web: in the Yarmill iOS app,
tap your avatar → **Connected Devices & Apps**. And for Garmin, enable both activity
and sleep in Garmin Connect so both flow.</Info>

## What imported data does — and doesn't

Imported records can't be edited or deleted in Yarmill (manage them in the provider's
app). And watch data doesn't replace the diary: you still write the left side and fill
the right side — the analytics use both.

## HR zones live in Yarmill

Time-in-zone is computed by Yarmill's own algorithm from the raw data, independent of
watch brand — so your history stays consistent even if you switch from one brand to
another. Set your zones in Yarmill, where two things are possible that watches don't
do:

- **Different zone sets per activity** — for example running vs. cycling.
- **Validity periods** — after a new lactate or spiroergometry test, a new zone set
  applies from its start date, while old activities keep the zones that were valid
  then. No retroactive rewriting of your history.
