Connect a device once and Yarmill takes it from there: recorded activities appear under the
right day in your [Training Log](/en/reality/training-log), and sleep and recovery data feed
the readiness [analytics](/en/analytics/analytics). You log once at the source, and the
numbers flow everywhere without re-entry.

**For:** athletes (device connections); admins (federation data) · **Where:** Web app; Apple Health from the iOS app

<Info>The wearable connectors and the heart-rate-zone settings below live on **your own**
Settings → Personal page — they're athlete-side. Coaches don't manage device connections from
their Settings; they read the imported data downstream. Which **team systems** and **results
databases** are wired up is configured per instance.</Info>

## Connect a device

Each athlete connects their own devices.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Applications & devices">
    Go to **Settings → Personal** and find the **Applications & devices** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Connect">
    Find your provider in the list and select **Connect**, then confirm the connection in the
    provider's own account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your Training Log">
    From the next sync, activities (and sleep, if you wear the device at night) appear
    automatically under the day.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A connector you've already linked shows **Disconnect** instead of **Connect**, so you manage
each connection yourself.

## Which devices you can connect

The Applications & devices list carries one row per service, each with a short description and
a **Connect** / **Disconnect** button. The connectors available to you depend on your instance;
they typically include:

- **Apple Health** — Apple Watch activity and health data.
- **Dexcom** — continuous glucose data.
- **Garmin** — Garmin sports-watch training data.
- **Oura** — Oura ring sleep and recovery data.
- **Polar** — Polar sports-watch training data.
- **Suunto** — Suunto sports-watch training data.
- **AC BALUO** — testing-lab results, shown in [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics).
- **WHOOP** — WHOOP band data.

<Info>**Apple Health connects from the iPhone**, not the web — the web can't grant the
permission. In the Yarmill iOS app, tap your avatar → **Connected Devices & Apps**. For
**Garmin**, enable both activity *and* sleep in Garmin Connect so both flow in.</Info>

## What imported data does — and doesn't

Once connected, recorded activities (and sleep and recovery, if the device tracks them) appear
under the right day in your [Training Log](/en/reality/training-log), below the day's entry.
Sleep and recovery data feed the readiness [analytics](/en/analytics/analytics) — the
sleep & recovery picture and the sporttester / WHOOP charts.

Imported records **complement** the diary — they don't replace it. You still write the left
side and fill the right side; the analytics use both. And imported records **can't be edited or
deleted in Yarmill** — manage them in the provider's app.

## HR zones live in Yarmill

Heart-rate zones are configured in the **Heart Rate Zones** section of the same
**Settings → Personal** page. Time-in-zone is then computed by Yarmill's own algorithm from the
raw device data, independent of watch brand — so your history stays consistent even if you
switch from one brand to another.

Zones are scoped **per discipline**, with **validity dates**, across bands **Z0–Z5**. Two
things are possible here that watches don't do:

- **Different zone sets per discipline** — for example running vs. roller-ski vs. ski.
- **Validity periods** — after a new lactate or spiroergometry test, a new zone set applies
  from its start date, while older activities keep the zones that were valid then. No
  retroactive rewriting of your history.

## Federation results databases

Beyond device connections, Yarmill can pull competition results from **federation results
databases** — for example IBU SIWIDATA (biathlon) or FIS. These auto-import into
[Results](/en/results/results), so the whole picture lives in one place.

<Info>Results-database connections are set up by an admin or by Yarmill — they're not athlete
self-serve — and each requires a signed license agreement with the data provider. Which
databases are available is configured per instance.</Info>

## How it connects

- Imported **activities** appear under the day in the [Training Log](/en/reality/training-log),
  alongside the diary entry.
- Sleep, recovery, and time-in-zone feed [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics) — recovery
  indicators, team daily readiness, and the sporttester / WHOOP charts.
- **Heart-rate zones** set on your Settings → Personal page drive the time-in-zone computation
  used across the analytics.
- **Federation databases** populate the [Results](/en/results/results) module.
