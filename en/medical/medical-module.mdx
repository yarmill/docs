The Medical module keeps the team's health picture in one place: who's injured or ill,
how it limits their training, and when they're expected back — with the full history
that makes injury patterns visible.

**For:** coaches and medical staff (per permissions) · **Where:** Web app

<Info>Medical data is sensitive. Who can see and edit it is permission-controlled and
configured per team.</Info>

## The team overview

**Injuries & Illnesses** opens with the group: each athlete's overall **status**, their
**open health problems**, and the **expected return to full training** — with overdue
returns flagged. It's the "who's available" answer at a glance.

## A health record

Select an athlete to see their records, split into **Open** and **Closed**. Each record
holds:

<ParamField path="Title & type">
  What it is, marked as an injury or an illness.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Properties">
  The record's state (**Open**/closed), its **training limitation** (for example
  **No training**), and the **responsible staff**.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Key dates">
  When it started, and the **expected return** date that feeds the team overview.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Note">
  Free-text context — circumstances, treatment, anything worth remembering.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Diagnosis">
  Coded against the **OSIICS** standard, so reporting stays consistent.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Files">
  Reports, imaging, and other documents attached to the record.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Activity">
  The automatic log of every change — who, what, when — plus timestamped comments.
</ParamField>

## Record a new problem

Select **+** (or **New quick entry** — shortcut `N`) and capture it in seconds: name it,
mark injury or illness, set the training limitation and expected return. Detail can
come later; the overview is useful immediately.

## Why keep it here

The training-limitation status flows into **Team daily readiness** in Analytics, so the
morning overview already knows who's out. And because records accumulate, recurring
problems become visible — the basis for prevention, not just availability.
