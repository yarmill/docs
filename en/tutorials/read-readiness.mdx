## Before you start
<Info>The board is only as full as the data behind it. For a complete picture you want athletes filling the [wellness questionnaire](/en/reality/wellness-questionnaire) each morning, recovery devices connected for those who wear one (see [Integrations](/en/platform/integrations)), and the [Medical module](/en/medical/medical-module) kept current — a missing training-limitation flag means an athlete reads as available when they aren't. **Team daily readiness** lives in [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics), which is configured per team; if you can't find it, the module may not be enabled or you may lack permission — ask your administrator.</Info>

## What you'll do
You'll open the readiness report for your group, read the three inputs for each athlete, and decide who to act on. By the end you'll be able to:

- Open **Team daily readiness** in Analytics and point it at your group.
- Navigate to today's overview.
- Read the three inputs per athlete — health status from Medical, device recovery, and wellness.
- Spot the flags and read the athletes' notes.
- Decide who to talk to and whose load to adjust before you plan.

## Steps
<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Analytics and pick Team daily readiness">
    Open the **Analytics** menu and choose **Team daily readiness** from the left report picker. It opens into the standard report layout — a report picker on the left, a **Filters** block, a period navigator across the top, and the report body below.
    {/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — Analytics menu open with Team daily readiness selected in the report picker */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick your group">
    In the **Filters** block, set the **Group** dropdown to the squad you're about to plan for. The board redraws with one row per athlete in that group.
    {/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — Group dropdown in the Filters block with a squad selected */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to today">
    Use the period navigator in the top bar to land on today's date, so you're reading this morning's self-reports and recovery — not history. Fill the questionnaire and check the board *before* training; readiness data is only useful ahead of the session.
    {/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — top-bar period navigator set to today */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the three inputs per athlete">
    For each athlete the row combines three things: the **health status** from the [Medical module](/en/medical/medical-module) — the training-limitation flag, **green** for full training, **amber** for modified, **red** for none; **device recovery** for athletes who wear a device (for example WHOOP recovery or Garmin Body Battery); and **wellness** from that morning's [questionnaire](/en/reality/wellness-questionnaire) (for example sleep, stress, mood, soreness, fatigue). Read them together — a green medical status with poor sleep and low recovery is a different morning than green across the board.
    {/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — one athlete row broken out into its three inputs: health status, device recovery, wellness */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Spot the flags and read the notes">
    Scan for the colors that need attention — amber and red training-limitation flags, and low recovery or wellness scores. The athletes' **notes** from the questionnaire are surfaced alongside, so when something's off ("slept badly, travel") you read it next to the numbers. Device columns are empty for anyone without a device connected — that's expected, not a problem.
    {/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — flagged rows highlighted with an athlete note surfaced beside the scores */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decide who to act on">
    Turn the read into two short lists: who to **talk to** (a red or amber medical flag, a worrying note, a sharp wellness drop) and whose **load to adjust** today (low recovery, accumulated fatigue). That's the board's job — it tells you where to look before you open Plan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## You're done
<Check>You've read the morning board and know who's ready, who needs a conversation, and whose load to ease. Carry that into your session: take it into [Plan](/en/plan/plan) and adjust today's training for the athletes the board flagged.</Check>
