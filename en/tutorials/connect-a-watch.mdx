## Before you start

<Info>
You'll need an account with the device provider you want to connect (for example a Garmin
Connect, Oura, or WHOOP account) and permission to grant Yarmill access to it. Each athlete
connects their own devices on their own Settings page — coaches read the imported data
downstream rather than managing connections. The connectors available to you are
**configured per team**, so your list may differ from the examples here. See
[Devices & integrations](/en/platform/integrations) for the full picture.
</Info>

## What you'll do

You'll connect one device, confirm its data is flowing, check your heart-rate zones, and read your recovery the next morning. By the end you'll have:

- a device connected on your **Settings → Applications & devices** page;
- activities (and sleep, if you wear it at night) landing automatically under the day in your [Training Log](/en/reality/training-log);
- your heart-rate zones set in Yarmill, so time-in-zone is computed consistently;
- recovery and sleep showing up in [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics) the next morning.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Applications & devices">
    Go to **Settings → Personal** and find the **Applications & devices** section. It lists
    one row per service, each with a short description and a **Connect** / **Disconnect**
    button.{/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — Settings → Personal with the Applications & devices list */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your provider">
    Find your provider in the list and select **Connect**, then confirm the connection in the
    provider's own account when prompted. A connector you've already linked shows
    **Disconnect** instead, so you can see at a glance what's active.

    <Info>**Apple Health connects from the iPhone**, not the web — the web can't grant the
    permission. In the Yarmill iOS app, tap your avatar → **Connected Devices & Apps**. The
    exact consent screen and return flow depend on the provider and are configured per
    team.{/* TODO(yarmill): verify the per-provider OAuth/consent return flow for the team's enabled connectors */}</Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm data is flowing">
    Imported data arrives **from the next sync** — the first one typically lands overnight, so
    if you've just connected, an empty day is expected rather than a problem. From then on,
    recorded activities (and sleep, if you wear the device at night) appear automatically under
    the right day in your [Training Log](/en/reality/training-log), below the day's entry.

    For **Garmin**, enable both activity *and* sleep in Garmin Connect so both flow in.
    {/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — Training Log day showing an imported activity below the diary entry */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your heart-rate zones">
    On the same **Settings → Personal** page, open the **Heart Rate Zones** section and confirm
    your zones. Yarmill computes time-in-zone with its own algorithm from the raw device data,
    independent of watch brand — so set your bands (**Z0–Z5**) here, scoped **per discipline**
    and with **validity dates**, rather than relying on the watch.{/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — Heart Rate Zones section with per-discipline bands */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read recovery and sleep the next morning">
    Open [Analytics](/en/analytics/analytics) and find the **Recovery indicators** report —
    recovery metrics from your connected device over the selected period (for example resting
    heart rate, HRV, sleep duration, or a device readiness score), each typically shown with a
    7-day rolling average. This needs a device you sleep with; without one the report is empty.
    Your recovery also feeds the **Team daily readiness** board.{/* TODO(yarmill): step visual — Recovery indicators report populated after an overnight sync */}
  </Step>
</Steps>

## You're done

<Check>Your recovery and sleep now populate automatically and feed the readiness board — one fewer thing to log, and one more signal in your morning picture.</Check>
