Reality is the training diary: what actually happened, recorded the same day while it's
fresh. It mirrors the Plan's structure — text on the left, numbers on the right — plus
the personal details only the athlete can supply.

**For:** athletes, coaches · **Where:** Web app and mobile

<Info>Yarmill is configured for each team, so the exact phases, fields, and data rows
you see may differ from this guide. The mechanics work the same way.</Info>

## A day in the diary

Open **Reality** and select the week. Each day card has the **left (text) side** —
for example a *daily goal*, *morning*, and *afternoon* phase — where you describe the
training in your own words. The **right (data) side** is the week's number grid:
sessions, time by activity and intensity, with calculated rows totalled for you.

Fields the coach doesn't plan but you should record — your instance might include, for
example, **RPE (0–10)** per phase — appear directly on the day. Everything auto-saves
as you type.

<Tip>Log the same day you train. Entries written a week later are guesses, and guesses
degrade your analytics — that's why the logging window is limited. On the **web** you can
backfill within a configured window (often around a week); the **mobile app logs the
current day only**, so write it on the day.</Tip>

## The day's ⋯ menu

The **⋯** menu on each day holds the extras. What's there depends on your instance —
for example: **notes**, **feeling scale**, **heart rate**, **mental condition**,
**attachment** (photos, videos, files), the **wellness questionnaire**, sport-specific
logs, and **import plan**.

## Import plan

If your team has it enabled, **import plan** copies the planned day into Reality with
one click — then edit it into what really happened. Some teams turn it off
deliberately, preferring athletes describe training in their own words.

## Watch data

If you've [connected a device](/en/platform/integrations), recorded activities — and
sleep, if you wear it at night — appear automatically under the day. Watch data
complements the diary; it doesn't replace filling in the left and right sides.

## Coaches in Reality

Coaches open an athlete from the left list to read and, with the right permissions,
write in their diary — commenting on notes, attaching video, or logging on the
athlete's behalf. Reality is per athlete: select a person, not the whole group.
